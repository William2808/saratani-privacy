SARATANI AI
Artificial Intelligence Policy
Responsible and Impactful Deployment of AI for Cancer Screening and Diagnosis
Executive Summary
Saratani AI is a Tanzania-based health technology startup pioneering the use of artificial intelligence to transform cervical cancer screening and diagnosis across Africa. As the first company in Tanzania to deploy AI specifically for this purpose, we operate at the intersection of cutting-edge technology and critical public health needs.
This AI Policy establishes the principles, obligations, and governance mechanisms that guide every aspect of how Saratani AI develops, validates, deploys, and monitors its AI systems. It applies to all staff, contractors, and partners involved in the design or use of our technology. Our policy is grounded in four foundational commitments: protecting patient safety and dignity, ensuring algorithmic fairness and transparency, maintaining rigorous regulatory compliance, and advancing equitable access to early cancer detection.
1. Scope and Applicability
This policy applies to:
All AI systems and machine learning models developed, licensed, or deployed by Saratani AI for cancer screening and diagnosis
All employees, researchers, clinical partners, and third-party contractors who interact with or contribute to these systems
All data collected, stored, processed, or shared in connection with Saratani AI's services
All geographies in which Saratani AI operates, with jurisdiction-specific requirements applied where applicable
2. Core Principles
The following principles form the ethical backbone of Saratani AI's approach to artificial intelligence. They are not aspirational; they are operational requirements embedded in our development and deployment processes.

Patient Safety First
No AI output will be used to make autonomous clinical decisions. All results serve as decision-support tools for qualified healthcare professionals.
Fairness & Non-Discrimination
Algorithms are developed and audited to ensure equitable performance across patient demographics, including age, ethnicity, geographic location, and socioeconomic status.
Transparency
The basis for AI recommendations will be explainable to both clinicians and patients in plain, accessible language.
Privacy by Design
Patient data is protected at every stage of the data lifecycle, from collection through deletion, using end-to-end security controls.
Accountability
Every AI-assisted decision can be traced, reviewed, and contested. Named individuals are responsible for each component of the system.
Continuous Improvement
Performance is monitored post-deployment, with regular re-validation cycles to maintain clinical reliability.


3. Ethical Use of AI
3.1 Avoiding Algorithmic Bias
Biased algorithms can cause direct patient harm by producing systematically inaccurate results for specific population groups. Saratani AI addresses this through the following measures:
Training datasets will be curated to ensure demographic diversity and geographic representativeness, with particular attention to the populations most affected by cervical cancer in Tanzania and the wider African region
Model performance will be disaggregated and reported across key subgroups (age, region, HIV status as relevant) at each validation stage
An independent bias audit will be conducted prior to any clinical deployment and annually thereafter
Identified biases will trigger a formal review process with documented remediation steps and timelines
3.2 Avoiding Technological Solutionism
AI is a tool, not a solution in itself. Saratani AI commits to:
Deploying AI systems only where they demonstrably improve upon or complement existing clinical workflows
Refraining from deploying systems in contexts where human capacity, infrastructure, or regulatory readiness are insufficient to support responsible use
Conducting pre-deployment feasibility assessments in each target environment
3.3 Human Oversight
AI recommendations are advisory. Healthcare professionals retain full clinical authority and responsibility for patient care decisions. Systems will be designed so that clinicians can override, question, or escalate any AI-generated output without penalty or friction.
4. Data Privacy and Security
4.1 Data Collection and Use
Patient data will be collected only for explicitly stated and consented purposes
Data will not be used for commercial profiling, sold to third parties, or repurposed without separate informed consent
Data minimization principles apply: we collect and retain only what is necessary for the intended clinical or research purpose
4.2 Security Standards
All patient data will be encrypted in transit and at rest using current best-practice encryption standards
Access to patient data will be role-based, logged, and regularly reviewed
Saratani AI will maintain a documented Incident Response Plan covering detection, containment, notification, and recovery in the event of a data breach
Annual penetration testing and security audits will be conducted by qualified third parties
4.3 Data Residency and Cross-Border Transfers
Where applicable, patient data will be stored within Tanzania in compliance with national data protection law. Any cross-border transfer of data, for example, to international research collaborators, will be governed by a data sharing agreement, anonymized where feasible, and subject to explicit regulatory clearance.
5. Informed Consent
Patient autonomy is a non-negotiable ethical requirement. Before any patient data is used for AI-assisted screening, analysis, or research, Saratani AI will ensure:
Consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
Patients are informed in plain language about: what AI is, how it is used in their care, the benefits and limitations of AI-assisted screening, and their right to refuse AI involvement without affecting the quality of their care
Consent materials are available in Kiswahili and other relevant local languages
Separate consent is obtained for the use of data in research or model training beyond direct clinical care
Patients have a clear, accessible mechanism to withdraw consent at any time
6. Transparency and Explainability
6.1 Clinician-Facing Transparency
AI outputs will include confidence scores and, where technically feasible, visual or textual explanations of the factors driving a recommendation
Clinicians will receive structured training on how to interpret, verify, and appropriately act on AI outputs
System documentation, including model architecture, training data provenance, and known limitations, will be maintained and made available to clinical partners
6.2 Patient-Facing Transparency
Patients will always be informed when AI technology is used in their screening or diagnosis
Patients may request a plain-language explanation of any AI output that influenced their care
6.3 Public Reporting
Saratani AI will publish an annual transparency report summarising model performance metrics, audit findings, incidents, and policy updates
7. Regulatory Compliance
Saratani AI will not deploy any AI system for clinical use until it has obtained appropriate regulatory approval from the Tanzania Medicine and Medical Devices Authority (TMDA) and any other relevant national or regional bodies. Our compliance framework includes:
Maintaining a regulatory affairs function responsible for tracking and responding to changes in applicable law
Documenting all validation studies, clinical evaluations, and post-market surveillance data in a format compatible with regulatory submission requirements
Conducting formal regulatory submissions before introducing new AI capabilities or expanding to new geographies
Actively engaging with regulators as a partner in shaping appropriate AI governance frameworks for healthcare in Tanzania
8. Accountability and Governance
8.1 Governance Structure
Saratani AI will establish an AI Ethics and Governance Committee comprising clinical, technical, legal, and patient representative members. This committee will:
Approve all new AI deployments
Review and respond to any bias audit findings or safety incidents
Oversee policy review and update cycles
Report to Saratani AI's Board of Directors on AI governance matters
8.2 Redress Mechanisms
Patients and clinicians who believe an AI-assisted decision has harmed or adversely affected care have the right to formal redress. Saratani AI commits to:
Providing a clear, accessible complaints process
Investigating all complaints within 30 working days
Where harm is substantiated, offering restitution, rehabilitation support, and a documented guarantee of non-repetition
Sharing anonymized learnings from substantiated complaints across the clinical team to drive systemic improvement
8.3 Responsibility Assignment
Named individuals identified in a separate internal register are accountable for model development and validation, clinical integration, data security, regulatory compliance, and post-deployment monitoring. These responsibilities are reviewed annually and updated upon any organizational change.
9. Monitoring, Evaluation, and Model Lifecycle
AI systems are not static products. Saratani AI treats post-deployment monitoring as a core operating function:
Performance metrics (sensitivity, specificity, false positive/negative rates) will be tracked continuously and reviewed monthly
Significant performance degradation, defined as a statistically significant drop below validated thresholds, will trigger an automatic review and potential suspension pending investigation
All models will undergo a formal re-validation cycle at least every 24 months, or earlier if there are material changes to target populations, clinical workflows, or data inputs
Retired model versions will be documented with clear records of why they were superseded.
10. Equitable Access
The clinical burden of cervical cancer in Tanzania falls disproportionately on women in rural, low-resource settings who lack reliable access to specialist care. Saratani AI's commercial and operational model must reflect this reality:
Pricing structures will include public-sector and community health facility tiers that make screening accessible regardless of facility budget
Technology design will accommodate low-bandwidth environments and devices available in resource-limited settings
Partnership agreements with government health systems will prioritize geographic coverage in underserved districts
Annual equity audits will assess whether uptake and outcomes are equitable across income, geography, and demographic groups, with findings reported to the Governance Committee
11. Education and Training
The safe and effective use of AI in clinical settings depends on practitioners' knowledge and confidence. Saratani AI will:
Provide mandatory onboarding training for all clinical users of the AI system prior to first use
Develop competency-based refresher training delivered at least annually
Produce plain-language educational materials on AI, its role in healthcare, and its limitations for distribution to patients and community health workers
Collaborate with medical schools and training institutions to integrate responsible AI literacy into health professional curricula
12. Intellectual Property and Licensing
Saratani AI will maintain clear, documented ownership of its proprietary algorithms, models, and associated training data
All third-party AI tools or datasets used in development will be governed by documented licenses that permit commercial healthcare deployment
Any collaborative research that results in jointly developed IP will be governed by a written agreement specifying ownership, licensing rights, and commercialization terms, agreed upon before the research commences.
Open-source contributions will be reviewed for license compatibility before incorporation
13. Sustainability, Scalability, and Risk Management
13.1 Sustainability
AI system design will account for long-term operational and maintenance requirements, not just initial deployment.
Cost-effectiveness evaluations will be conducted alongside clinical performance assessments
13.2 Scalability
Architecture decisions will ensure that systems can be scaled to the national or regional level without fundamental re-engineering
Expansion to new countries or clinical indications will require a formal readiness review against regulatory, clinical, and technical criteria
13.3 Risk Management
A risk register covering technical, clinical, regulatory, and reputational risks will be maintained and reviewed quarterly
Contingency plans for system outages, data breaches, or significant model failures will be documented and tested annually
Business continuity plans will ensure that clinical care is not disrupted by the unavailability of the AI system
14. International Collaboration
Saratani AI recognizes that advancing AI for cancer screening in Africa requires collaboration beyond our borders. We commit to:
Actively participating in relevant international bodies and working groups on AI in healthcare
Sharing de-identified research findings, performance benchmarks, and policy learnings with peer organizations and academic institutions
Adopting and contributing to globally recognized standards for AI in medical devices, including those developed by WHO, ISO, and regional health authorities
Ensuring that international data-sharing arrangements protect the interests, privacy, and sovereignty of Tanzanian patients
15. Policy Review and Version Control
This policy is a living document. It will be:
Reviewed in full by the AI Ethics and Governance Committee at least annually
Updated promptly in response to material changes in regulation, technology, organizational structure, or clinical evidence
Versioned with each update, with a change log maintained to document what was modified and why
Made available to all staff, clinical partners, and — in summary form — to the public

Our Commitment
Saratani AI is committed to ensuring that the benefits of artificial intelligence reach the patients who need them most, with the safety, dignity, and autonomy of every patient at the center of all we do. This policy is the foundation of that commitment. We will uphold it, enforce it, and evolve it as the science and the standards demand.
